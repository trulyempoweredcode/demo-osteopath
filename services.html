What I Treat

Services

Osteopathy treats a wide range of musculoskeletal conditions. Explore the areas where my patients see the most significant, lasting improvement.

Back Pain & Sciatica

Back pain is the most common reason people visit an osteopath, and for good reason. Whether you're dealing with a sudden sharp episode that's locked you up, or a dull ache that's been grinding away for months, osteopathic treatment can help identify the root cause and get you moving again.

Sciatica — pain that radiates down the leg from the lower back — often responds well to hands-on treatment. I use a combination of soft tissue release, joint mobilisation, and gentle manipulation to reduce nerve irritation, restore movement, and relieve pressure on the affected structures.

  • Lower back pain and stiffness
  • Sciatica and leg pain
  • Disc-related problems
  • Sacroiliac joint dysfunction
  • Chronic and recurrent back pain

Neck Pain & Headaches

Neck pain is incredibly common, especially among people who spend long hours at a desk or looking down at screens. Tight muscles, stiff joints, and poor posture can all contribute to pain that radiates into the shoulders, arms, and even the head. Many tension headaches and cervicogenic headaches originate from the neck.

I work through the muscles and joints of the neck, upper back, and shoulders to release tension, improve range of motion, and address the underlying postural habits that keep the problem coming back. Treatment is always gentle and tailored to your comfort level.

  • Neck stiffness and restricted movement
  • Tension headaches and migraines
  • Whiplash-related injuries
  • Trapped nerves and referred arm pain
  • Jaw pain and TMJ problems

Joint & Muscle Pain

Pain in the shoulders, hips, knees, and other joints can develop gradually through overuse, poor mechanics, or age-related changes. Muscle strains, tendon irritation, and ligament stress all respond well to osteopathic treatment. The key is understanding which structures are involved and why they're under strain.

I assess how the whole body moves — not just the painful area — to identify compensatory patterns and underlying causes. Treatment typically combines soft tissue work, joint mobilisation, and targeted exercises to restore balance, reduce inflammation, and build long-term resilience.

  • Shoulder pain and frozen shoulder
  • Hip pain and bursitis
  • Knee pain and runner's knee
  • Tennis elbow and golfer's elbow
  • Muscle strains and repetitive strain injuries

Sports Injuries

Whether you're a seasoned athlete or a weekend warrior, injuries happen. From hamstring tears and ankle sprains to overuse injuries like shin splints and Achilles tendinopathy, osteopathic treatment can speed up your recovery and help you get back to the activities you enjoy.

As a keen cyclist myself, I understand the frustration of being sidelined by injury. I focus on not just treating the injury itself, but also identifying the biomechanical factors that led to it — so you can return to sport stronger and more resilient than before.

  • Hamstring and calf strains
  • Ankle sprains and instability
  • Shin splints and plantar fasciitis
  • Achilles tendinopathy
  • Post-injury rehabilitation and prevention

Posture & Workplace Health

Modern working life takes a real toll on the body. Hours spent hunched over a laptop, sitting in poorly set-up chairs, or standing in one position can lead to chronic pain in the back, neck, shoulders, and wrists. Many of my patients are surprised at how much their daily habits contribute to their symptoms.

I combine hands-on treatment to relieve the immediate pain with practical, straightforward advice on workstation setup, movement breaks, and corrective exercises. The goal is to break the cycle of pain, stiffness, and poor posture so that your body can cope with the demands of your working day.

  • Desk-related back and neck pain
  • Rounded shoulders and upper back tension
  • Wrist and forearm strain (RSI)
  • Ergonomic and workstation assessment
  • Postural correction exercises

Pregnancy & Postnatal

Pregnancy brings enormous changes to the body — shifting your centre of gravity, loosening ligaments, and placing new demands on your spine and pelvis. Lower back pain, pelvic girdle pain, and rib discomfort are extremely common, particularly in the second and third trimesters. Osteopathic treatment during pregnancy is safe, gentle, and highly effective at managing these symptoms.

After birth, the body needs time to recover. Postnatal osteopathy helps address lingering aches, pelvic floor recovery, and the postural strain of feeding and carrying your baby. I use gentle techniques adapted to each stage of pregnancy and recovery, always prioritising your comfort and safety.

  • Pregnancy-related back and pelvic pain
  • Pelvic girdle pain (PGP/SPD)
  • Rib and mid-back discomfort
  • Postnatal recovery and realignment
  • Feeding-related neck and shoulder strain
