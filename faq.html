Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about osteopathy and appointments with me. If your question isn't here, get in touch.

Osteopathy is a form of manual therapy that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of musculoskeletal problems. Osteopaths use their hands to identify areas of restriction, tension, or imbalance in the body, and treat them using techniques like soft tissue massage, joint mobilisation, and manipulation. It's a regulated healthcare profession in the UK, and all practising osteopaths must be registered with the General Osteopathic Council.

No, you don't need a referral from your GP to see an osteopath. You can book directly with me. However, if you have a complex medical history or are currently under investigation for a condition, it can be helpful to let your GP know you're seeking osteopathic treatment. I'm always happy to communicate with your GP if needed.

Wear whatever you're comfortable in. I may need to examine and treat the area that's causing you trouble, so loose-fitting clothing or sportswear works well. For some assessments, I may ask you to remove outer layers so I can observe your posture and movement properly. You're welcome to bring shorts or a vest top if you'd prefer. Your comfort and dignity are always a priority.

It depends on the condition, how long you've had it, and how your body responds to treatment. Many patients notice improvement within two to three sessions. After your initial consultation, I'll give you an honest estimate of how many appointments I think you'll need. I don't believe in booking unnecessary sessions — my aim is to get you better as efficiently as possible.

Most patients find treatment comfortable and even relaxing. Some techniques — particularly deep tissue work on tight muscles — can cause mild discomfort, but I'll always check in with you and adjust my approach. It's normal to feel a bit achy for a day or two after treatment, similar to how you might feel after exercise. This usually settles quickly and is a sign that your body is responding.

Yes, many headaches — particularly tension headaches and cervicogenic headaches (those originating from the neck) — respond very well to osteopathic treatment. I'll assess your neck, upper back, and shoulders to identify any tension or joint restriction that may be contributing. Treatment typically involves gentle mobilisation and soft tissue techniques, combined with advice on posture and ergonomics.

Pregnancy places significant demands on the body, and osteopathy can help manage the aches and pains that often come with it — particularly lower back pain, pelvic girdle pain, and hip discomfort. I use gentle, safe techniques that are adapted for each stage of pregnancy. Many of my patients find regular treatment throughout pregnancy helps them stay comfortable and mobile.

I ask for at least 24 hours' notice if you need to cancel or reschedule your appointment. This allows me to offer the slot to another patient who may be waiting.
