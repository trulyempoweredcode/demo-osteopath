Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

1. Introduction

This privacy policy explains how Tom Richardson Osteopathy collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal information when you use this website or engage my services. I am committed to protecting your privacy and handling your data in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Data Controller

The data controller responsible for your personal data is:

Tom Richardson Osteopathy
9 Park Lane
Leeds, LS1 3BE
Phone: 07700 900 207

3. What Information I Collect

I may collect and process the following personal data:

  • Contact information: your name, email address, phone number, and postal address when you contact me via the website, phone, or email.
  • Health information: details about your musculoskeletal health, medical history, and clinical records that you provide during consultations, which is classified as special category data under UK GDPR.
  • Clinical notes: assessment findings, treatment notes, and exercise prescriptions recorded during and after your appointments to support your ongoing care.
  • Website usage data: information about how you use this website, including your IP address, browser type, and pages visited, collected through cookies and analytics tools.
  • Payment information: transaction records (I do not store card details directly).

4. How I Use Your Information

I use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide information about my services.
  • To provide osteopathic treatment and maintain accurate clinical records as required by the General Osteopathic Council.
  • To manage appointments and send appointment reminders.
  • To process payments for services rendered.
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations, including GOsC standards of practice.
  • To improve this website and your experience of it.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

I process your personal data on the following legal grounds:

  • Consent: where you have given clear consent for me to process your personal data for a specific purpose, such as submitting a contact form.
  • Contract: where processing is necessary for the performance of a contract with you, such as providing osteopathic treatment.
  • Legitimate interests: where processing is necessary for my legitimate business interests, such as improving my services, provided these do not override your rights.
  • Legal obligation: where processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation, including requirements set by the General Osteopathic Council.
  • Explicit consent: for special category data (health and musculoskeletal information), I rely on your explicit consent, which you may withdraw at any time.

6. Data Storage & Protection

Your personal data is stored securely and I take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against unauthorised access, loss, or damage. These measures include:

  • Encrypted digital storage for electronic clinical records.
  • Secure, locked storage for any paper records.
  • Password-protected devices and systems.
  • Regular review of data security practices.

Your data is stored within the United Kingdom and is not transferred outside the UK unless necessary and with appropriate safeguards in place.

7. Data Retention

I retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected:

  • Clinical records: retained for 8 years after your last appointment, in line with GOsC professional guidelines and insurance requirements.
  • Contact form submissions: retained for up to 12 months if you do not become a patient.
  • Website analytics data: retained for up to 26 months.

After the retention period, your data will be securely deleted or anonymised.

8. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access: you can request a copy of the personal data I hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: you can request that I correct any inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: you can request that I delete your personal data, subject to legal and professional obligations.
  • Right to restrict processing: you can request that I limit how I use your data.
  • Right to data portability: you can request a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object: you can object to the processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, you can withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact me using the details in section 2. I will respond to your request within one month.

If you are not satisfied with how I handle your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

9. Cookies

This website uses cookies to improve your browsing experience and analyse site traffic. For full details about the cookies I use and how to manage them, please see my Cookie Policy.

10. Changes to This Policy

I may update this privacy policy from time to time to reflect changes in my practices or legal requirements. Any significant changes will be communicated through this page. I encourage you to review this policy periodically.
